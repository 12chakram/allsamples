UltraViolet (Beta)Privacy Policy

The UltraViolet service and the accompanying Web sites and user interfaces (collectively, "UltraViolet") are provided by Digital Entertainment Content Ecosystem (DECE) LLC and its affiliates (collectively, "DECE"). UltraViolet provides a digital rights locker solution which gives registered members of an UltraViolet household ("Household") one place to access to all of the UltraViolet-compliant digital content (the "Digital Content") purchased or rented by the Household. UltraViolet also provides registered users of a Household account (each, a "Member") the right to manage access to the Digital Content in the Household account, and the right to register or update different UltraViolet-compliant devices for the playback of the Digital Content (each, a "Device").

This privacy policy (the "Privacy Policy") sets forth DECE's information collection, use and disclosure practices with regard to UltraViolet.

You may opt in or opt out of DECE's sharing of your Personal Information with third parties for marketing purposes by following the directions in the "Right to Opt In and Opt Out" [Embed hyperlink] section below.

UltraViolet is intended for Members who are located in the United States of America. This Privacy Policy shall be interpreted under the laws of the United States.

Purpose and Scope of this Policy

Protecting the privacy of Personal Information is very important to DECE. This Privacy Policy explains, in general, what Personal Information and other information is collected through UltraViolet, how the information is used, and with whom DECE may share such information. "Personal Information" means information that may identify Members as individuals or allow online or offline contact with Members as individuals, including, for example, your name; display name; username; physical address; e-mail address; telephone number; photo or chosen image; age; date of birth, security questions and answers; parental controls; individual demographic information; Digital Content purchase or rental information, including, without limitation, title, artist, date of purchase, and place of purchase; information regarding your Digital Content streams and usage; the number of, and the makes and models of, the Devices you have added to the Household account; and the number of Members registered to the Household account and their Personal Information.

Right to Opt In and Opt Out

You may have the right to "opt in" to certain of DECE's uses and disclosures of your Personal Information. For example, at the time you create the Household, and anytime thereafter by accessing the Household account, or through the Web site of a retailer providing UltraViolet-compliant Digital Content (a "Retailer"), you may have the opportunity to opt in to (1) allowing Retailers to access and display to you the Digital Content associated with the Household (including Digital Content that you obtained from another Retailer), (2) allowing Retailers to market to you based on the contents of the Household account, (3) receiving promotional offers from DECE, and (4) receiving third-party offers. Once you opt-in, you will then have the right to change your mind and opt-out.

Please understand that, even if you opt-out of receiving promotional correspondence from DECE, DECE and its authorized service providers may still contact you in connection with your relationship, activities, transactions and communications with DECE and the Household account. Also, if you wish to stop receiving promotional messages from third parties with whom DECE has already shared your contact information, you will need to contact them directly.

Information Automatically Collected ("UltraViolet Usage Information")

Cookies

DECE may use "cookies" to keep, and sometimes track, information about you. Cookies are small data files that are sent to your browser or related software from a Web server and stored on your computer or Device. Cookies track your activities and preferences while using UltraViolet. They may, for example, store your username and/or password. A cookie may enable DECE to relate your use of UltraViolet to other information about you, including your Personal Information. All of these purposes serve to improve and personalize your experience using UltraViolet, including this Site.

Most Web browsers can be set to inform you when a cookie has been sent to you and provide you with the opportunity to refuse that cookie. Additionally, if you have a Flash player installed on your computer, your Flash player can be set to reject or delete Flash cookies. However, refusing a cookie may, in some cases, preclude you from using, or negatively impact the display or function of, UltraViolet or certain areas or features of UltraViolet. By using UltraViolet, you consent to DECE's use of cookies as described above.

Clear GIFs

DECE may use "clear GIFs" (aka "Web beacons" or "pixel tags") or similar technologies to enable DECE to know whether you have visited a Web page, viewed content, or received a message. A clear GIF is typically a one-pixel, transparent image (although it can be a visible image as well), located on a Web page or in an e-mail or other type of content or message, which is retrieved from a remote site on the Internet enabling the verification of an individual's viewing or receipt of a Web page, content or message. A clear GIF may enable DECE to relate your viewing or receipt of a Web page, content or message to other information about you, including your Personal Information.

IP Address, Clickstream Data, and Device Information

DECE's servers automatically collect data about your Internet address when you use UltraViolet. This information, known as an Internet Protocol address, or IP Address, is a number that's automatically assigned to your computer or Device by your Internet service provider or carrier whenever you're online. DECE's servers may also record the Web page from which you linked to the Web Site (e.g., another Web site or a search engine); the pages you visit on the Web Site; the Web site you visit after the Web Site; the ads you see and/or click on; other information about the type of Web browser, computer, Device, platform, related software and settings you are using; any search terms you have entered on the Web Site or a referral site; and other usage activity and data logged by DECE's servers. DECE uses this information for internal system administration, to help diagnose problems with DECE's servers, and to administer UltraViolet. Such information may also be used to gather broad demographic information, such as country of origin and Internet service provider or carrier. DECE may also link this information with your Personal Information.

Any or all of these activities with regard to UltraViolet Usage Information may be performed on DECE's behalf by DECE's services providers.
